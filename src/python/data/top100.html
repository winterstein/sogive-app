America’s Top 100 Charities
  • FORBES
EDITED BY WILLIAM P. BARRETT
DECEMBER 16, 2021, 06:00 PM
  • U

    nder the shadow of Covid-19, the nation’s top 100 charities reported a 10% increase in private donations during their most recently reported fiscal years, collectively receiving $54.4 billion in support. That’s one of the highest percentage increases in the 23 years Forbes has been compiling this list, and stands in sharp contrast to last year’s performance, when donations to the top 100 fell 4%.

    The surge in giving was, in part, a rare positive side effect of the pandemic. In early 2020, as the country locked down and entered a steep recession, Americans who still had jobs found themselves awash in federal stimulus cash and moved by the plight of those who weren’t so lucky. Notably, the No.2 charity, Feeding America, a network that supplies 200 food banks nationwide, reported a 26% jump in gifts (mainly donated food) and No. 3 Salvation Army posted a 21% increase. The stock market and large donors also contributed to strong giving, experts say. The tax code makes it particularly advantageous to donate appreciated stock and after its initial Covid crash, the market rebounded, with the S&P 500 index posting a 16% gain in 2020, on top of a 29% advance in 2019.

    Our list is based on the most recently reported fiscal year for each charity. As in previous years, the rankings reflect private contributions only; government grants and payments for services aren’t including in the ranking, though each organization’s total revenue is noted. The full list below can be sorted by the category of mission—for example, domestic needs, international needs, health or environmental. Each organization’s name links to a separate page providing even more data, including how much it pays its top executives. United Way Worldwide, a network with more than 1,300 local chapters, again ranked No. 1, bringing in $3.85 billion in its fiscal year ended this past June 30, a 7% increase. While United Way has traditionally been sustained by paycheck deductions, 45 of its chapters received gifts from MacKenzie Scott, just named by Forbes as the World’s Most Powerful Woman for her groundbreaking efforts to give away her $57 billion fortune.

    The cutoff for this year’s list—No. 100—is $167 million, 10% above last year’s $152 million. Among the most notable first-timers is the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson’s Research, which debuts at No. 84, with $200 million in donations. Fox stands out among single-illness charities for the combination of its significant research, its high charitable commitment ratio (the percent of total expenses spent on the stated mission, as opposed to fundraising or other overhead) and its high fundraising efficiency (the percent of donations remaining after fundraising costs).
